01 The Problem

The Failure Loop Frustrating Developers

The Founders noticed a growing group of frustrated developers who were trying to build AI agents in OpenClaw. Developers were stuck in a "Failure Loop" & their AI agents would forget what they were doing mid task, "shatter" during complex workflows, and require hours of prompt tweaking for tiny results.

Even using powerful AI like Claude, the agents themselves were "dumb" because they had zero identity and zero consistency.

02 Research & Engineering

Engineering Their AI Startup on Scientific Foundations

Rather than guess, the founders grounded their product in peer reviewed research, three methodologies that meaningfully changed how AI agents behave.

ExpertPrompting — Xu et al. 2023
Expert Identity Matching
We know that giving generic labels to AI like "you are a copywriter", had zero measurable improvement and those generic labels leave the AI with "zero identity" and "zero consistency". The ExpertPrompting Method included automatically synthesizing customized, paragraph-long descriptions of a distinguished expert suited to a specific task. Identities include specific beliefs, opinions, and even "productive flaws" that cast one precise character for that task. Founders claim this method worked.
Tree of Thought — NeurIPS 2023
Structured Deliberation at Scale
Founders claim the Tree of Thought framework improved performance by 3 to 5 times, enabling agents to deliberate across branching reasoning paths rather than committing blindly to a single chain of thought.
Metacognitive Prompting — NAACL 2024
Five-Stage Self-Aware Evaluation
Inspired by the human's 5 self aware evaluations: Text Interpretation, Preliminary Judgment, Critical Evaluation, Final Decision, and Confidence Assessment. Founders claim a performance boost of 27%.
03 Building & Development

Vibecoded from Research to Production

Founders Vibecoded the entire app and used Tailwind CSS and React for the frontend, and Anthropic (Claude) on the backend.

Now agents could actually browse, register, and download tools programmatically via an API, transforming what was once a brittle, hand held process into a coherent, autonomous workflow.
